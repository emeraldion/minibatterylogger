User Preferences

MiniBatteryLogger lets you to customize about every aspect of the appearance and the behavior of the application.

To show the Preferences window, choose Preferences from the MiniBatteryLogger menu.

The General pane lets you customize the active probe interval, and enable or disable the visualization of the current battery status in the application’s Dock icon and menubar.

The probing interval is the time between two forced polls of the battery data. If set to Never, the application will passively wait for a change in the power properties, resulting in less CPU usage. When you’re on battery you can safely ignore this setting.

Checking the button Launch as Login Item, the application will be automatically launched at startup.

Check Automatically save sessions and Save session on quit to ensure that monitoring data are saved to disk even if you don’t manually save them.

The Chart View pane lets you customize the colors of the charts, enable or disable the amperage graph and the display of event bubbles.

Color is fundamental when dealing with information. If you don’t like the default colors of the chart or you feel more comfortable with a dark background, you can do it from here.

You can also enable or disable the display of those little colored bubbles that mark a power event — oh no, please! They’re so cute!

The Alerts pane lets you customize the threshold under which Low battery and Battery Dying alerts are issued.

To customize the behavior of notifications, refer to the MiniBatteryLogger entry under the Applications list of Growl System Preferences pane.

The Software Update pane lets you enable or disable the automatic update check during the application startup. It also shows the date of the last update check.

Updates are handled by the Sparkle framework .

A convenience button allows you to manually check for updates.

The Sharing pane lets you decide the sharing policy of MiniBatteryLogger. MiniBatteryLogger communicates with a Shared Battery Data Archive, a huge database of battery statistics on the web, both sending and retrieving data to be used to rate users’ batteries.

You can choose wheter the app should automatically send your battery data to the shared archive, fetch shared data for your laptop model or not (of course, you will always be able to do it manually from the Comparison View.

Additionally, starting from 1.7, MiniBatteryLogger features a network battery data sharing service. This will make computers running MiniBatteryLogger, MiniBatteryStatus or the standalone battd daemon monitorable in an iTunes-like fashion.

Remote batteries will be automatically presented in the left sidebar just like local ones. You will be able to perform on them all the operations you would normally do on a local battery. System administrators will love this feature. Learn how the sharing service works.
