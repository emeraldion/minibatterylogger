Using MiniBatteryLogger

MiniBatteryLogger is a utility to monitor your laptop’s battery.

When you open it for the first time, you’ll be presented the Chart View. Here it is plotted the chart of the charge and the amperage of the battery along time.

Using the view selector you can jump to the other three views: the Statistics View, the Comparison View and the Log View.

MiniBatteryLogger also shows the current battery status in its Dock icon, so you can safely hide its window when you’re busy doing something else, still keeping the battery under control. A replacement status item for the menubar with enhanced functionality is also provided.

To quickly realize what are the current conditions of the selected battery, there’s a nice Battery Status view, with the percentage of charge always visible. The colored bar decreases in width with the battery charge, and ranges from green (full charge) to red (empty), assuming all the intermediate colors. still keeping the battery status under control.

The Battery Status view can be collapsed when not needed using the toggler on the lower bar or the menu item under the View menu.

For your convenience, the app has also a menu item that summarizes the current battery conditions. You can safely hide the app still keeping the battery status under control.

Additionally, you can enable Growl notifications for events of interest (e.g. to be informed when the charge has gone under a given amount).

A typical MiniBatteryLogger workflow consists in monitoring the battery during a full discharge and/or a recharge, then saving the session data for later analysis. You can disable the monitoring when you’re not interested in charting or logging, but just want to see how much charge is left.

Another way to keep an eye on the battery conditions is to show the Desktop battery.

The Desktop battery shows the conditions of the battery currently being monitored. It can be dragged around and, since it stays below all windows, will never be in your way.

The Desktop battery is hidden by default and can be enabled by the Preferences.

The Battery Inspector shows in detail every aspect of your battery in real time. Information is grouped by type: Details, health and times.

The details section shows the charge in percentage, power status (plugged, charging), the values of amperage (current and peak value), voltage, capacity (current, maximum and original).

The health section shows the number of battery cycles, that is, the number of times the battery has been fully discharged and recharged to full.

The times section shows the estimated times to discharge and to full recharge, when appropriate. The times section also includes a convenient counter that increases only when you’re not plugged to AC power, with a reset button. It is really useful to know how long you’ve been running on your battery.
